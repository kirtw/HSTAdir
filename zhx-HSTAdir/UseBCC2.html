Use BCC for player list


In gmail and all email, there are three standard fields to designate who will get the email, ie to whom it is addressesd and who it will be sent to.
There is a TO: field, a CC: field and a BCC: field.

The last, BCC, stands for Blind CC. For those of you under the age of 65, CC is the traditional abbreviation for sending a paper "carbon copy" to additional people.
The blind list did not get typed on the original, thus "blinded" the recipients to who was on the list to receive a carbon copy.

The Rule: Always use the BCC field to put the list of players to whom you want to send an email.

Put ResHSTA itself in the TO: field.  This places a marker in the inbox.

To populate the bcc field, on your desktop (not the gmail app on your phone) click on to: to make the cc and bcc boxes appear if they are not visible.
Click the bcc label itself at the left end of the bss field.  (sic, not my design- why so obscure??? Hover says )   This takes you to the contact list.
Now click in the right upper quadrant the dropdown "My Contacts" and Select the "Invitation List".
Now click Select All checkbox in the left upper quadrant.
Then, finally the "Insert" button at the right lower corner.
This should pop you back to your email being composed with 96 or so emails in the bcc box.
         You can selectively delete some if you know of a problem with some, but should let me know if this gives any bad entries.
Be careful at this point, it is easy to send prematurely. I usually do this step last so the gun is not loaded while typing. Sending an erroroneous partial message is more embarrassing when it goes to 96 people.

WHY?

First it prevents noise when one person inadvertently replies-to-all. You have all experienced this and it further enables the hate mail exchanges that follow.

Second is to prevent the whole list from falling into spammers hands. An email does not contain the whole list from the bcc field, but it does when the list is put in either the to: or cc: field. If any one member's email gets hacked, the automatic bots immediately scrape up emails with lists of email addresses and these get used or sold to spammers and we all get more spam.

Unfortunately, when you click the email envelope icon within Google Contacts, it puts the selected list in the TO: field and does not give you an easy choice of bcc: You can, however, select-all (ctrl/A) the emails in the to:, cut them out (ctrl/x) and then paste them into the bcc: box (ctrl/v). Be Careful. It is easy to accidentally send this prematurely.

There is no way to create a contacts email list from the gmail-app. You have to use the desktop-browser mode to do this. Do not ask me why. I have Googled it and it is a feature. So use the desktop and browser to set up the Invitations email or others for multiple recipients.
